Client Support Specialist

Position Description:

The Delaware Community Foundation (DCF) seeks an experienced assistant with both sales support and research experience who has distinguished him/herself through his/her strong customer relations skills to support the expansion of the Foundation’s Funds development. This is a full-time position with a competitive salary, excellent benefits, and great working conditions.

The Delaware Community Foundation is among the top ten percent of the nation’s private not-for-profit community foundations (out of a field than now exceeds 750 community foundations) with approximately $240 MM in charitable funds under management through more than 1,200 funds. Since 1986, the DCF has been assisting individuals and nonprofit organizations to build endowed, scholarship, donor advised and designated funds; facilitating philanthropy, and has providing community leadership in Delaware.

The Client Support Specialist will support the development team, reporting directly to the Senior Vice President for Development, and will also provide direct support services for the Foundation’s President & CEO. This position will also be responsible for coordinating the continuous improvement of relationships with DCF clients.

The ideal candidate for this position will be an experienced administrator who has the skills to utilize various research tools to expand the organization’s knowledge about its current fundholders and its potential clients. Additionally, the successful candidate will have the organizational skills, the data management experience, the discretion, and the attention to detail to be able to maintain the organization’s database and customer relationship management software in ways that will make it easier for all staff and volunteers to access and use actionable information about its clients.

While some aspects of the position’s responsibilities may be routine, a talent for creative problem solving, the ability to recognize/respond to both unique challenges and opportunities, and experience with relational databases are absolutely essential traits for success in this position. A willingness to take ownership of the fulfillment of client needs will be one of the most important characteristics of the successful candidate for this position.

Key Responsibilities:

  • Maintain client and prospect files by continually updating and enriching information in individual records in our Foundation database (FIMS â a MicroEdge product specifically designed for community foundations) and in our CRM (based on Salesforce) prospect management system.
  • Manipulate and utilize data to provide essential management reports and specialized lists for mailings and other communications with fundholders, clients and prospects.
  • Utilize advanced research tools including WealthEngine, Google Search, LinkedIn and professional databases to identify and qualify prospects. Utilize more basic research tools like Switchboard, Instant Checkmate, county property and tax records, lists of donors, etc. to locate and update contact information on current and potential clients.
  • Assist the President & CEO, the Senior Vice President, and the DCF development team through the creation, production and mailing of annual fund request materials. Prepare reports for management on gifts received, and guarantee that all gifts are promptly and personally acknowledged.
  • Utilize the Customer Relationship Management and primary database system to track contacts and relevant information on current and prospective clients in the organization’s “pipeline” to facilitate the development team’s work.
  • Interact with potential new fundholders during initial contacts, handling requests as necessary and engaging senior members of the development team as appropriate. This individual will become sufficiently adept and conversant with the DCF’s products and services to fully service some DCF clients. Such services may include (but will not be limited to) providing information on the Foundation and different kinds of funds, and helping to open smaller funds.
  • Assist current fundholders with highly personalized service. Field questions and respond with accurate, complete and helpful information in a timely way as a means of building each client’s confidence in and appreciation of the DCF.
  • Assist as necessary with the planning, preparation, and execution of all key fundholder and prospect events, activities and communications.
  • Assist with the preparation of presentations, support documents, fund agreements, fundholder profiles, and materials to be used in prospect and organizational meetings.

Qualifications and Experience:

The successful candidate must be self-motivated, flexible, friendly, upbeat and comfortable working independently as well as part of a team. He/she must have a demonstrated competency and creativity in utilizing sources and methods to research people. He/she must have a demonstrated competency in office administration and data management. Given the customer-service oriented culture of the Foundation and its ambitious growth goals, the successful candidate must be able to multi-task, set priorities, and remain upbeat, accommodating and flexible in a client-centric and community-focused environment.

Fluency with Word, PowerPoint, Excel, Salesforce, Internet searches, social media, and databases will be a strong plus.

Please email resume and cover letter outlining applicable experience, including research, administrative, and technical skills, to dfleming@delcf.org by June 6, 2014.

The Delaware Community Foundation is an equal opportunity employer that seeks a diverse pool of candidates.

Telephone inquiries are strongly discouraged. No recruiters please.
