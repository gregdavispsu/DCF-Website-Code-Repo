Doing More for Delaware:
Resources & Examples

We are expanding our work to become an even greater community resource by strategically leveraging our organizational and fiscal strength, our expertise in philanthropy, and our standing in the community.

But what exactly does that mean? It means that we aspire to:

  • Become a more community knowledge and data-focused organization
  • More more effectively assess and address evolving issues
  • Serve as a source of knowledge, data and insight about community needs
  • Apply this knowledge to increase service to our donors and the community

During the past year, we have been studying how much other community foundations are accomplishing – above and beyond creating and managing charitable funds. We are inspired, and we want to increase our work so we can increase our impact.

Below are examples of projects other community foundations have taken on that might serve as models for the DCF in the future:

> Erie Vital Signs 2014
