About the Fund for Women
Created in 1993, the Fund for Women at the Delaware Community Foundation is a permanent endowment and additional source of funding for programs that address the unmet needs of women and girls in Delaware. The original goal of the Fund was to obtain 1,000 Founders, each contributing $1,000 to create a $1 million lasting endowment.

More than 1,000 men and women have become Founders, and due to their generosity and fundraising success, the endowment now exceeds $2.8 million. The Fund for Women includes the Fund for Women Leadership Fund, as well as the Mary E. Copper Memorial Fund, Lynn Estes Friess Fund, Sally J. Knox Memorial Fund, Clara Miller Trust Fund, Betsy Rawls Fund, and the Marjory Stone Memorial Fund.

Fund for Women Grants

The Fund tor Women focuses its grant making on programs relating to the education of girls and women in health, wellness and life skills that lead to future economic self-sufficiency. Grant awards have supported:

 

  Housing Job training
  Abuse counseling Financial literacy
  Legal assistance Arts enrichment
  Medical care Science education
  Scholarships

Sports programs

  Prenatal care  


Grant applications are available in November and due in January each year. Awards are announced in May. In May 2013, 12 nonprofit organizations received a total of $128,904 in grants.
