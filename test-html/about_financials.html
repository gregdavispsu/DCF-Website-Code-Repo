Financials
The Delaware Community Foundation promotes and administers charitable endowment funds to benefit the people of Delaware. We strive to provide the highest level of stewardship possible to our fund donors.

The DCF Investment Committee of the Board is responsible for recommending and implementing the Foundation's investment policy. In addition, the Investment Committee may adopt special investment policies for individual endowment funds with assets up to $5 million each and $20 million in aggregate, which require different provisions. The Investment Committee delegates certain responsibilities to professional experts including investment managers, a custodian and an investment consultant. 

The Foundation currently utilizes the services of ten primary professional money management firms, each of which has a specific objective and is evaluated against a market index. The DCF benchmark index is a composite of these standard indexes, weighted to match our investment mix. This allows us to evaluate both the absolute and relative performance of our money managers.

Our Investment Management of Delaware Community Funds brochure includes listings of the DCF's Investment Committee and DCF Investment Managers along with our current endowment asset allocation. March 2013 brochure now available!
